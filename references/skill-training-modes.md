# Skill Training Modes

Use this reference when designing listening, reading, speaking, or writing lessons.

## Listening

Choose intensive listening when the learner needs accuracy.

Intensive loop:

1. Preview keywords.
2. Listen to a short chunk.
3. Dictate or reconstruct.
4. Compare with transcript.
5. Mark missed words, linking, reductions, pauses, and chunks.
6. Shadow or repeat aloud.
7. Retell the meaning.

Choose extensive listening when the learner needs exposure, interest, and intuition.

Extensive loop:

1. Pick material that is interesting and not too hard.
2. Listen for main idea.
3. Extract a few reusable expressions.
4. Retell or discuss.

## Reading

Choose intensive reading when the learner needs depth.

Intensive loop:

1. Predict topic.
2. Identify main idea.
3. Track paragraph logic.
4. Explain key expressions.
5. Rewrite or simplify hard sentences.
6. Summarize in English.

Choose extensive reading when the learner needs stamina.

Extensive loop:

1. Choose manageable material.
2. Limit dictionary use.
3. Track enjoyment and completion.
4. Save only high value words.

Avoid translating the whole text first when the goal is reading skill.

## Speaking

Use short cycles.

Speaking loop:

1. Ask one prompt.
2. Learner speaks or drafts for 1 to 3 minutes.
3. Fix 1 to 2 important problems.
4. Give one useful expression.
5. Learner repeats the same answer immediately.

For exam speaking, simulate pressure and use rubric based feedback.

For daily speaking, build reflex phrases and short natural responses.

## Writing

Writing loop:

1. Learner writes first.
2. Mark the top issues.
3. Learner revises.
4. Show a stronger version for comparison.
5. Extract reusable patterns.

For email or workplace writing, provide tone variants after the learner has a draft.

For exam writing, separate task response, coherence, vocabulary, and grammar.
