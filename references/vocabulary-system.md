# Vocabulary System

Use this reference when vocabulary is the goal, bottleneck, or review target.

## Principles

Vocabulary should be learned through context and use. A word meaning alone is not enough for long term mastery.

For beginners, simple meaning lookup is acceptable. For ongoing learning, require examples, collocations, learner sentences, and review.

## Card Fields

Use these fields for a vocabulary record:

1. Word or expression.
2. Meaning.
3. Pronunciation note or audio need.
4. Example sentence.
5. Common collocations.
6. Confusing words or near misses.
7. Source material.
8. Learner sentence.
9. Review status.
10. Next review date.

## Review Schedule

Use practical checkpoints:

1. 5 minutes.
2. 1 day.
3. 2 days.
4. 4 days.
5. 7 days.
6. 15 days.

For short sessions, create only the next checkpoint. For plans, create the full schedule.

## Practice Types

Mix formats:

1. Meaning recall.
2. Collocation choice.
3. Fill in the blank.
4. Error correction.
5. Learner sentence creation.
6. Short dialogue using target words.
7. Retell a short passage with target expressions.

## Learner Style

Visual learners may benefit from cards, layout, color, and mental images.

Auditory learners need pronunciation, listening, and read aloud practice.

Always include pronunciation exposure when a word will be used in speaking or listening.

## Word Selection

Prioritize:

1. High frequency words.
2. Words from the learner's real material.
3. Collocations and reusable expressions.
4. Words that block comprehension.
5. Words the learner repeatedly forgets.

Avoid filling the session with rare words unless the user is preparing for an advanced exam or technical domain.
